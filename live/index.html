SAP

Join the Slack channel now: #partner-sap

#1 An ERP for Individuals

SAP is one of the leading providers of ERP solutions for large companies. Your challenge is to create a vision of an ERP for individuals. If everyone was able to easily capture their personal supplies and demands and match them to what others need or have to offer (of course while maintaining data privacy), a lot of benefits could emerge:

  • Think about small farmers and large food companies. Individually, their negotiation positions are highly unbalanced. But if every farmer had their own ERP and their data could be aggregated e.g., by district, county, or country, the playing field can be leveled.
  • Another application are crisis situations like in refugee camps or during natural disasters. There, knowing which demands exist, which supplies, and which skills are available in a timely manner is crucial.

Feel free to focus on any aspect of this wide topic ranging from frontend design for the individual ERP all the way up to data aggregation and analysis from potentially hundreds of thousands of individual ERPs.

#2 Analytics for Transparent Politics

SAP has a long-standing history in the Analytics field. We enable companies to create full transparency about their business. Your challenge is to provide the same kind of transparency for politics.

In the upcoming European elections, citizens need to decide which politicians best present their political views. Campaign advertisements and even Wahl-O-Mat like tools provide only a very high-level aggregation of a party‚Äôs or politician‚Äôs views. It‚Äôs their day to day decisions and voting behavior that might paint a much more telling picture.

Help citizens figure out who to vote for by, e.g., collecting and matching previous votes in parliaments, campaign donation information, etc. To analyze and visualize the data, you can request a free trial account for SAP Analytics Cloud, if needed.

#1 Enable users to detect fake news using a smart service

Fake news spreads fast on the internet, and keeps coming back again and again. Define fake news for the purposes of your solution and pick one or more approaches to detect it: e.g., a style-based, credibility-based, propagation-based, and/or knowledge-based approach. Devise a service classifying text or web addresses as fake news by using your definition and approach(es). Technologies may include but are not limited to natural language processing, graph analysis, sentiment analysis, time series analysis. Integrate your service on mobile, e.g., by offering your service via the share actions from other apps so that individuals reading a message can check suspicious content.

#2 Visualize cross-border effects innovatively

Reality sometimes refuses to be sorted into the categories we would like to see it in. For example, political and social analyses are often done on country or province level. But when diseases break out, when peoples move around the globe, when terrorist groups spread from village to village, they are not restricted to national borders.

Focus on such an example and design an innovative way of visualizing its effects over time, bringing together what is actually happening with how decision-makers wish to analyze it. Your solution should smartly transform geospatial data towards a fancy visualization. Your solution could empower decision-makers at international crisis centers to better understand latest developments in order to coordinate humanitarian help. Which parts of which countries are affected how strongly over time? How flexible can your borders be?

Labour Rights

With almost 1,400 projects in more than 80 countries around the globe Bread for the World is helping poor and marginalized people to improve their living conditions by their own efforts.

In South Eastern Europe, the rights of women working in the textile industry are often still trampled underfoot: disrespectful treatment; intimidation; limited or prohibited use of toilets; illegal excessive and unpaid overtime; reported cases of women who should sign in their contracts that they will not become pregnant during the next two or more years. Out of fear for their own jobs, workers hardly report grievances and are afraid to claim their rights. Little knowledge and information about labour rights, almost no trade union representation and restrictions on trade union organizing in many of the companies are further limitation factors to claim rights.

Your challenge is to develop a digital solution that encourages and enables the workers to inform themselves about their labour rights quickly and easily and to report reliable cases of labour rights violations anonymous. Your solution would be a big step forward for those affected to be empowered to exercise their rights!

Organic Food in Cameroon

With almost 1,400 projects in more than 80 countries around the globe Bread for the World wants to help poor and marginalized people to improve their living conditions by their own efforts.

Like many African countries, Cameroon has a very young population structure: 62% of the population is younger than 25 years. This means that hundreds of thousands of people enter the labour market every year, even though the country is already struggling with high unemployment. The agricultural sector offers employment opportunities, but has for a long time been unattractive, especially for young people.

We are training young farmers who produce organic certified food. But they have to find a new market for their products (chicken, various vegetables, eggs, pork, fruit etc.).

Your Challenge is to create a digital solution to bring producers, consumers and carriers together to manage in an App the offers and the prices of several products. The problem: As production is not constant throughout the year, the producers must stay in contact with potential buyers who buy their products promptly at harvest and are willing to pay a higher price than for conventionally produced products.
